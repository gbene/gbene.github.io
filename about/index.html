About

I am 27 years old with a passion for science, technology, nature, photography and all trades that involve getting my hands dirty! I love coding and I love geosciences so I do both! I am a avid supporter and advocate for open-source and open-science, we would not be here without both!

Currently I am pursuing a PhD in Geophysics at the University of Iceland. My work focuses on simulating sequences of repeating earthquakes observed at the Bárðarbunga volcano using Sequences of Earthquakes and Aesismic Slip (SEAS) simulations (in particular using the Structural Boundary Integral Method).

Previously I have occupied a Reseach assistant position for 1.5 years at the University of Milano-Bicocca, studying unbiased statistical modelling for fracture lengths. You can read about it here!

Before that, I completed my MSc in Geology and Geodynamics at the Univeristy of Milano-Bicocca. My thesis consisted in contributing to the open-source 3D-geological modelling software PZero by implementing the pointcloud visualization and processing module.

I have also worked here and there as a freelance programmer.

In my free time I like to hike, swim (icelandic pools are awesome!), play guitar and cook. I recently started to learn how to sail, do pottery and bake bread!
